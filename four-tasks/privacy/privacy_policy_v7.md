# Privacy Policy — Four Tasks

**Last updated:** 11 May 2026
**Last reviewed:** 11 May 2026
**Version:** 7

This is the privacy policy for **Four Tasks**, a habit-tracking app made by **THE PICKLE MOON** (Morgan Ryan, ABN 92 231 512 645). Contact us at **thepicklemoon@gmail.com** for any questions about this policy or the data we hold.

This policy is written in everyday language. We don't think privacy should require a law degree, and we don't want to hold your data the way most other companies do.

THE PICKLE MOON is a small Australian business. Although we're below the AU$3 million annual turnover threshold that automatically makes a business an "APP entity" under the Privacy Act 1988, we voluntarily apply the Australian Privacy Principles to how we handle your data. Everything below describes what that means in practice.

---

## What we collect

When you use Four Tasks, we store the following on our servers so the app works:

- A **username** you choose (any text — not your real name unless you decide to use it).
- The **username of your partner** in the app, which you also type in. Holding both pieces of information helps us connect the two of you and recover your account should we need to.
- The **four task labels** you set up — whatever you wrote in the boxes. These can change as often as you like.
- The **daily status message** you pick or compose using the in-app message picker. These change daily by default, and hopefully serve as a fun feature to look back upon.
- **Which days you marked tasks as done**, and which tasks on those days.
- **Which days you marked as rest days.**
- The **coin count** the app keeps for you.
- Your **install date** — the day you first logged in. This helps us render your calendar from the first day, instead of populating lots of empty months going backwards in time.
- A few small **settings flags** — your chosen emoji icons, whether you've opted out of the leaderboard, and similar in-app preferences.
- **Subscription state** — whether your account currently has an active subscription, the renewal date, which app store the subscription came from (Apple or Google), and whether you're currently in a free trial or grace period. We do not see card details, billing addresses, or any other payment information — Apple and Google handle that part directly.
- **Bug reports** you submit through the in-app bug catcher, including any text you type.

That's the whole list.

## What we don't collect

We want to be specific about this because it's the more interesting list. We have no interest in gathering data that isn't voluntarily given. We don't want to hold any data that isn't directly relevant to the way the app functions.

- **No real names, email addresses, phone numbers, or postal addresses.**
- **No card numbers, billing addresses, or payment details.** Apple and Google handle the entire transaction. Your payment details go to them, never to us. We receive only a notification telling us whether your subscription is active.
- **No IP addresses, device identifiers, or advertising IDs are stored in your record.** (Our hosting provider sees IP addresses at the network level, as any web service does, but we don't keep them or attach them to you.)
- **No location data.**
- **No third-party tracking inside the app.** We don't embed Google Analytics, Facebook SDK, Mixpanel, or any other library that tracks how you use the app. (Apple and Google show us anonymous, aggregated install and crash data through their developer dashboards — that's not the same thing, and it's covered by their privacy policies, not ours.)
- **No advertising.** The app shows no ads and we don't share data with advertisers.

If you don't tell the app something about yourself directly (by typing it in or by subscribing), we don't have it.

## Where your data is stored

Four Tasks stores data on infrastructure provided by **Cloudflare**, which operates a globally distributed network. Your data may be stored at any Cloudflare data centre worldwide, including locations outside Australia.

We don't sell, lease, or share your data with any third party. Cloudflare hosts the data on our behalf — they're a hosting provider, not a partner. Cloudflare's own data handling is governed by their privacy policy, available at **cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/**.

By using Four Tasks, you acknowledge that your data may be stored and processed outside Australia. THE PICKLE MOON takes reasonable steps to ensure that any overseas storage handles your data consistently with the Australian Privacy Principles.

## How we protect your data

All data sent between Four Tasks and our servers travels over encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS). Your data is stored on Cloudflare's infrastructure, which uses industry-standard encryption at rest. Access to the database is restricted to THE PICKLE MOON — no one else has a key.

We follow the principle of least data: we don't ask for what we don't need, and we don't store what we don't need.

No system is perfectly secure. If we discover a data breach that's likely to harm you, we'll let you know directly and notify the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC), as required by the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme under Australian law.

## Subscriptions and payment

Four Tasks is a subscription product. The subscription is processed entirely through **Apple** (on iOS) or **Google** (on Android) — not through us.

What this means for your privacy:

- **We never see your payment information.** Card numbers, billing addresses, and payment dates are handled by Apple or Google. They send us a small notification telling us your subscription is active or has changed; that's all.
- **We store only what's needed to know whether you can use the app.** A subscription status flag, a renewal date, the originating store, and trial/grace period state.
- **You manage your subscription through Apple or Google**, not us. To cancel, change, or get a refund, use:
  - **iOS:** Settings → your name → Subscriptions
  - **Android:** Google Play → Profile → Payments and Subscriptions → Subscriptions
- **If you reinstall the app or switch devices**, the app will check with Apple or Google to recover your subscription — no action needed from you beyond signing into the app store you originally subscribed through.

We can see whether you're currently subscribed. We can't see what you paid, when you paid, or how. If you'd like a refund, contact Apple or Google — we don't process refunds and don't have the ability to.

## How long we keep your data, and how to delete it

Your data stays on our servers for as long as you use the app. There are three ways it can be deleted:

1. **You delete it from inside the app.** There's an in-app option to permanently delete your account and all associated data. When you confirm, your record is removed from our servers immediately. There's no soft delete and no archived backup — once you delete, the data is irrecoverable, by design.
2. **You ask us to delete it.** Email **thepicklemoon@gmail.com** with the username you used. We'll delete your record within 7 days and email you to confirm.
3. **You cancel your subscription and stop using the app.** Your record stays where it is unless you also use one of the methods above. We don't proactively delete inactive users at this stage.

When we delete your record, we delete everything associated with it — task labels, completion data, status messages, coin counts, settings, bug reports, and subscription state. We don't keep "soft-deleted" copies for any reason except where the law specifically requires us to (which is currently nowhere — but if that ever changes, this section will be updated).

## Who can see your data

- **You** — through the app on your device.
- **Your partner in the app** — they can see your task completion and status message because that's the point of a partner app.
- **Us** (THE PICKLE MOON) — when we look at the database to fix bugs or investigate problems. We don't read individual user data for any other reason.
- **Cloudflare** — at the technical level required to store and serve the data.
- **Apple or Google** — only your subscription information, only as needed to handle billing. They never see your task labels, status messages, or other in-app data.
- **Nobody else.**

If we add a public leaderboard feature, only your username, install date (for comparison's sake) and coin count are shown to other users, and you can opt out at any time via the in-app long-press on the coin display.

## Children

Four Tasks is not designed for or directed at children under 13. We don't knowingly collect data from anyone under 13. If you believe your child under 13 has used the app and submitted data, email us at **thepicklemoon@gmail.com** and we'll delete the record.

## Your rights

Under Australian privacy law (the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles), and broadly equivalent laws in other countries:

- **You can ask what we hold about you.** Email us at **thepicklemoon@gmail.com** with your username and we'll send you everything we've got.
- **You can ask us to delete your data.** Use the in-app deletion option, or email us.
- **You can ask us to correct something.** Most fields are editable inside the app directly. For anything else, email us.
- **You can cancel your subscription at any time** through Apple or Google (see "Subscriptions and payment" above). Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period.
- **You can complain.** If you think we've handled your data badly, you can complain to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at **oaic.gov.au**.

## If you're outside Australia

Four Tasks is available worldwide. Wherever you live, the same principles apply: we collect what's listed above, store it on Cloudflare, share it with no one, and delete it when you ask.

If you're in the **European Union or United Kingdom**, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including the right to data portability and the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. To exercise any of these rights, email **thepicklemoon@gmail.com**.

If you're in **California**, you have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to know what we hold about you, request deletion, and opt out of the sale of your data. We don't sell data, so the third applies by default. Email **thepicklemoon@gmail.com** to exercise the others.

The legal basis we rely on for processing your data is the performance of our contract with you (you've installed the app, we run the app, that requires storing your data) and your consent (you typed it in).
